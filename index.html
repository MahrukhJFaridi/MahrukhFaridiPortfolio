MS Power BI Project Dashboard

Wellness and Life-style Survey Breakdown

This Power BI project conducts a detailed analysis of a wellness and lifestyle survey involving nearly 16,000 respondents. Through meticulous data scrutiny, it uncovers insightful findings about participants' lives. Notably, it highlights a gender disparity in work-life balance, with women demonstrating a stronger equilibrium between professional and personal spheres compared to men. Moreover, the survey reveals that men experience higher levels of daily stress, with 75% reporting stress compared to 25% of women. Age emerges as a crucial factor shaping behaviors, with older individuals showing a greater inclination towards charitable giving, while the younger demographic engages more with social media. Additionally, the survey unveils a notable aspect of financial satisfaction, with an impressive 80% of respondents expressing contentment with their current salaries.

Tableau Project Dashboard

Vehicles Sales Data Analysis

This Tableau project offers a deep perspective on various aspects by conducting an extensive analysis of automobile sales data. At the forefront, Ford emerges as the dominant player in the automotive market, both in terms of revenue generation and sheer volume of cars sold. One of the key insights presented is the comparison between the average market price of cars and their actual selling prices, shedding light on pricing dynamics and consumer behavior. The dashboard also delves into the preferences of consumers regarding car colors, identifying the hues that are most popular among buyers. State-wise breakdown of market prices provides valuable geographical insights, showcasing regional variations in car pricing trends. Web Scraping Project Using Python

Extracting World Obesity Data

This project focuses on analyzing and extracting world obesity rates for both men and women through web scraping with Pandas. After inspecting the webpage's HTML structure, we meticulously extracted data using Python. Initially, we cleaned and structured the extracted titles, then created a data frame for storage. Utilizing a for loop, we systematically extracted row and column data and inserted it into the data frame. Following the same process, we created a separate data frame for female obesity rates. Finally, we merged both data frames to compile a comprehensive dataset for further analysis.
